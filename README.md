A Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Graph Neural Network based on Wavelet-enhanced and Harmonic Memory for Long-term Prediction of Sea Surface Core Variables

ABSTRACT

Reliable long-term forecasts of key sea-surface variables are essential for global marine weatherrouting and navigation support, offshore operation scheduling, and other ocean engineering
applications. The sea surface temperature, sea surface salinity, sea water velocity and sea surface wind stress have complex multi-scale cross-domain coupling relationships, long-distance
temporal correlations, and dynamic spatial dependencies. Due to the difficulty in handling these complex and dynamic spatiotemporal relationships, long-term forecasting often leads to a
decline in field pattern consistency. This paper proposesWavelet-enhanced Harmonic Memorybased Spatio-Temporal Adaptive Graph Neural Network (WaveHMSTGNN), which integrates
variable-level wavelet gated fusion module, harmonic fusion memory unit, and signed sparse adaptive graph convolution module within a unified spatio-temporal graph prediction framework.
These components are respectively employed for multi-scale representation enhancement, stable long-term recursive update, and structured spatial dependency learning. The experiment based
on the HYCOM data shows that this method outperforms 11 advanced models in terms of 7-day full-time effectiveness, and significantly improves the consistency and long-term stability under
the longest time period. Transfer experiments in the South Atlantic and Central Pacific further indicate strong generalization potential across heterogeneous oceanographic environments,
suggesting the model’s value as an environmental forecasting component for marine operational decision-support systems.
